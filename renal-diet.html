Kidney Health Food Guide

Goal: Low Sodium, Low Potassium, Low Phosphorus.
Tailored for seniors. Consult a doctor before changes.
🍗 Proteins
🟢
Good (Eat Freely)Egg whites, White fish (Cod), Skinless Chicken.
🟡
Caution (Portions)Beef, Whole eggs, Tofu, Pork.
🔴
AvoidBacon, Sausages, Deli meats, Ham.
🥦 Vegetables
🟢
Good (Eat Freely)Cauliflower, Onions, Garlic, Cabbage, Peppers.
🟡
Caution (Portions)Peas, Asparagus, Potatoes (Must be double-boiled).
🔴
AvoidTomatoes, Spinach, Pickles, Olives.
🍎 Fruits
🟢
Good (Eat Freely)Apples (peeled), Blueberries, Strawberries, Grapes.
🟡
Caution (Portions)Pears, Peaches, Plums.
🔴
AvoidBananas, Oranges, Kiwis, Raisins.
☕ Drinks & Flavoring
🟢
Good (Eat Freely)Water, Ginger tea, Cranberry juice, Herbs, Lemon.
🟡
Caution (Portions)Rice milk, Unsalted butter, Honey.
🔴
AvoidSalt, Lo-Salt, Cola, Orange juice, Soy Sauce.

⚠️ Critical Rules

1. NO SALT SUBSTITUTES: Do not use products like 'Lo-Salt'. They are pure potassium and can stop the heart in kidney patients.

2. DOUBLE BOIL: Peel and boil potatoes twice in fresh water to remove potassium.

3. NO ALCOHOL: Stick to water or cranberry juice.
